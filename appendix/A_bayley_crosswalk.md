# Appendix A — Bayley Crosswalk

This appendix maps each of the 26 bonds biographized in this book to its corresponding section in Rafael A. Bayley's *The National Loans of the United States, From July 4, 1776, to June 30, 1880* (Government Printing Office, 1881). Bayley's section titles, chapter numbers, and approximate page locations are given for the digital MyST edition of the book maintained at `bayley-myst/paper/ch0X_*.md`.

When you read a passage in this volume that is attributed to Bayley by chapter and section title, this table tells you which Bayley file to open to find the original.

## Notes on the mapping

- **Bond IDs** are the L1 identifier in the Hall–Payne–Sargent Bond Database (`BondList[bond_id]`).
- **Bayley section titles** are reproduced verbatim from the digital MyST edition. Some Bayley sections cover multiple bonds (e.g., the "Six Per Cent., Three Per Cent., and Deferred Six Per Cent. Stocks of 1790" covers Bond IDs 20021, 20022, and 20023; the "Funded Loans of 1881, 1891, and 1907" covers Bond IDs 20120 and 20121).
- **Page numbers** are approximate locations in the original 1881 PDF; in the MyST edition, you can navigate by section anchor (`(sec-...)=`) or by line number in the corresponding `paper/ch0X_*.md` file.
- **Bayley's recapitulation tables** (`paper/ch05_recapitulation.md`) provide tabulated issues and redemptions for most of the bonds in this book; consult the recapitulation chapter for raw numerical data per fiscal year.

## Sections in Bayley not biographized in this book

The 26 bonds in this volume are a representative cross-section, not an exhaustive treatment. Among the major Bayley sections **not** biographized here are:

- The Holland Loans of 1782, 1784, 1787, 1788, 1790, 1791, 1792, 1793, 1794 (foreign Revolutionary borrowing).
- The Subscription Loan of 1791, the Six Per Cent Stock of 1796, and the Navy Six Per Cent Stock.
- The Eight Per Cent Loans of 1798 and 1800 (Quasi-War financing).
- Most of the smaller War of 1812 loans (e.g., the Ten Million Loan of 1814, the Six Million Loan of 1814).
- The various Treasury Notes of 1837–1845 (small-scale antebellum emergency paper).
- The 5% Loan of 1881, the Loan of 1858, the Loan of 1860, and the Loan of February 1861.
- The Refunding Certificates, Certificates of Deposit, Silver Certificates, and various non-interest-bearing currency instruments documented in Bayley ch. 4.

Readers interested in any of these may consult the Bayley digital edition directly. The Hall–Payne–Sargent database also includes time series for many of these issues, supporting future biographies in the same template if desired.
