### Statutes (selected)

Citations in the form `12 Statutes, 345` follow Bayley's convention and refer to the *United States Statutes at Large*. Major acts referenced repeatedly in this book:

- **Funding Act of August 4, 1790** (1 Statutes, 138). Established the Six Per Cent, Three Per Cent, and Deferred Six Per Cent Stocks.
- **Independent Treasury Act, August 6, 1846** (9 Statutes, 59). Established the subtreasury system.
- **Legal Tender Act, February 25, 1862** (12 Statutes, 345). Authorized the Five-Twenties of 1862 and the first greenbacks.
- **Act of March 3, 1863** (12 Statutes, 709). Authorized the Loan of 1863, One Year Notes, Two Year Notes, and Compound-Interest Notes.
- **National Banking Act, February 25, 1863** (12 Statutes, 665). Created the National Banking System and the federal-bond reserve requirement.
- **Act of June 30, 1864** (13 Statutes, 218). Authorized the Five-Twenties of June 1864.
- **Act of March 3, 1865** (13 Statutes, 468). Authorized the Five-Twenties of 1865 and the Consols.
- **Public Credit Act, March 18, 1869** (16 Statutes, 1). Mandated coin redemption of pre-1869 federal bonds.
- **Funding Act of July 14, 1870** (16 Statutes, 272). Authorized the 4%, 4.5%, and 5% refunding bonds.
- **Specie Payment Resumption Act, January 14, 1875** (18 Statutes, 296). Restored the gold standard effective January 1, 1879.
